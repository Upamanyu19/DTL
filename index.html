About MedWaste Connect

The rapid expansion of clinical facilities requires an equally robust framework for biomedical waste management. MedWaste Connect bridges the gap between healthcare providers and authorized disposal vendors.

Our platform acts as a secure aggregator, ensuring that hazardous materials—from pharmacological waste to sharps—are tracked, managed, and disposed of according to strict CPCB guidelines. By digitizing vendor discovery and contracting, we allow clinicians to focus on patient care while maintaining total environmental compliance.

Biomedical Waste Segregation Guidelines

Strict adherence to molecular and toxicological safety standards begins at the source. Understand the categories below.

Yellow Category

Contents: Human anatomical waste, animal waste, soiled waste, expired/discarded medicines, chemical waste, and clinical microbiology waste.

Disposal: Incineration or deep burial.

Red Category

Contents: Contaminated waste (recyclable) such as tubing, bottles, intravenous tubes and sets, catheters, urine bags, syringes (without needles).

Disposal: Autoclaving, micro-waving, followed by shredding.

White Category (Sharps)

Contents: Needles, syringes with fixed needles, scalpels, blades, or any other contaminated sharp object that may cause puncture and cuts.

Disposal: Dry heat sterilization followed by shredding/mutilation or encapsulation.

Blue Category

Contents: Broken or discarded glass, vials, and ampoules (except those containing cytotoxic wastes).

Disposal: Disinfection (by soaking in sodium hypochlorite) or autoclaving, followed by recycling.
